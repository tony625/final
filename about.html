~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

This program allows you to compare individual stocks and find relevant information about each one. I created it so that I could easily compare new positions with my existing ones so I wouldn't make rash investment decisions. I like to focus on dividend and long-term investments. I want stable positions that I can passively manage and can keep through my working lifetime. I've chosen a few indicators that will help me identify fair value in a stock. I've set some personal benchmark values below:


A Price to Earnings Ratio (PE) of less than 50.

A Beta (the measure of volatility) below 1.5

A Market Capitalization above $100 Billion (Large Cap)

A Dividend of 1% or more


These are not hard and set rules, rather, they act as guides for my investments. The information panel as shown below also includes many other indicators to show short term movement in the market. they are useful, though not critical to my investment strategy. To compare stocks, input two ticker symbols in the boxes at the bottom of the page. To compare stocks to the benchmark simply put in a ticker in just the first input box.
